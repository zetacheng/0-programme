# Research Workflow

## Programme-level workflow

1. Identify the owning paper repository and authoritative source.
2. Register any cross-paper dependency as `PROPOSED` or `UNVERIFIED`.
3. Specify a falsifiable programme gate only when more than one paper is affected.
4. Lock assumptions, conventions, required evidence, and the kill criterion before work begins.
5. Keep derivations, scientific code, raw outputs, and detailed gates in the owning paper repository.
6. Require reproducible evidence and independent review before acceptance.
7. Record the reviewer verdict and PI decision separately.
8. Synchronize accepted status into every affected repository and update `SYNC_STATUS.md`.
9. Preserve failures, inconclusive outcomes, and superseded routes.

## Evidence boundary

Conversation history is not verification. Programme summaries link to evidence; they do not replace evidence.
